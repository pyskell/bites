Essentially Lightning is a faster type of Bitcoin transaction, that rather than being sent directly to the Bitcoin network is sent directly to friends, family, and merchants. Later they may choose to move the BTC they received to the Bitcoin network.

When Lightning is mature it will be usable for reasonably sized transactions and purchases between friends and merchants while the Bitcoin network will remain the preferred method for large transactions.

{% endblock at_a_glance %} {% block status %}

Lightning is in alpha to beta state which means it works most of the time but may have bugs. Lightning wallets continuously improve in security and quality. The leading Lightning wallets are generally simple and easy to use.

At the moment you should only use small amounts of BTC on Lightning due to risks related to the early nature of the Lightning ecosystem.

{% endblock status %} {% block future %}

When Lightning is mature it will be usable for most reasonably sized transactions between friends and merchants and you won't need to worry about your wallet monitoring the Bitcoin network because they all will.

Most importantly when Lightning is mature you may not even know you're using it, you'll just open your wallet and pay. Your wallet will then make a smart decision on how to send the BTC, whether that be on Lightning, on the Bitcoin network, or on another payment system. Some Lightning wallets already do this today.

Of course Bitcoin tech is all about freedom, so if you want to take more fine-grained control over your money you'll be able to do that as well.

{% endblock future %} {% block vs_alts %}

Lightning is best thought of as Bitcoin's version of a credit card network (like Visa or Mastercard). With this in mind Lightning will enable an experience that's like a credit card with much more privacy. An important part of Lightning is that its design and development effort is going into making sure that when you buy something no one else is storing your entire shopping history. Today many companies collect our data and use it manipulate us into purchasing things we don't need and then sell our data to others who really shouldn't have it. Lightning will make it so that only the buyer and seller know each other and no other parties have access to the transaction. In that way Lightning (and Bitcoin) is much more like cash than a credit card. {% endblock vs_alts %} {% block further_reading %}
