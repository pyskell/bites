Bitcoin has the hardest tech

Bitcoin is not flashy, nor hyped tech. Bitcion is tech that gets serious work done well. Unfortunately this approach often leads to the erroneous and deceptive belief that Bitcoin is technologically archaic, or worse has no development.

As a former Bitcoin newbie myself I can tell you that I spent years viewing Bitcoin as old tech, rather than as tech focused on a singular and vital goal: a native internet currency, that may one day bring to money what the Internet brought to freedom of speech.

Below you will find an ever-incomplete list of emerging Bitcoin tech that serves as a stepping off point for understanding the advancements and freedoms that Bitcoin may one day bring all of us.

Please also keep in mind that this is tech explained simply, as such it will gloss over specific technical details. If you're excited by these things and want more in depth and accurate information then please refer to the "Further reading" tabs.

Lightning

A way to send Bitcoin in seconds between friends and merchants

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Essentially Lightning is a faster type of Bitcoin transaction, that rather than being sent directly to the Bitcoin network is sent directly to friends, family, and merchants. Later they may choose to move the BTC they received to the Bitcoin network.

When Lightning is mature it will be usable for reasonably sized transactions and purchases between friends and merchants while the Bitcoin network will remain the preferred method for large transactions.

Lightning is in alpha to beta state which means it works most of the time but may have bugs. Lightning wallets continuously improve in security and quality. The leading Lightning wallets are generally simple and easy to use.

At the moment you should only use small amounts of BTC on Lightning due to risks related to the early nature of the Lightning ecosystem.

When Lightning is mature it will be usable for most reasonably sized transactions between friends and merchants and you won't need to worry about your wallet monitoring the Bitcoin network because they all will.

Most importantly when Lightning is mature you may not even know you're using it, you'll just open your wallet and pay. Your wallet will then make a smart decision on how to send the BTC, whether that be on Lightning, on the Bitcoin network, or on another payment system. Some Lightning wallets already do this today.

Of course Bitcoin tech is all about freedom, so if you want to take more fine-grained control over your money you'll be able to do that as well.

Lightning is best thought of as Bitcoin's version of a credit card network (like Visa or Mastercard). With this in mind Lightning will enable an experience that's like a credit card with much more privacy. An important part of Lightning is that its design and development effort is going into making sure that when you buy something no one else is storing your entire shopping history. Today many companies collect our data and use it manipulate us into purchasing things we don't need and then sell our data to others who really shouldn't have it. Lightning will make it so that only the buyer and seller know each other and no other parties have access to the transaction. In that way Lightning (and Bitcoin) is much more like cash than a credit card.

Lightning: Keysend

Send Bitcoin to your friends by username

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Keysend (formerly Sphinx Send) is a way to store your friend's and family's public keys and send them Bitcoin. Your friend sends you an invoice, your wallet takes some data out of it and saves it to your phone with a nickname you choose.

It's in development and has recently made its way into a piece of Lightning software called LND. Lightning enthusiasts are using it to send payments to each other.

Once stable it'll be a commonly used contact list

Rather than publicly available solutions that give your username to the entire world (like Venmo or Paypal) Keysend is private and personalized so you might send Bitcoin to "Mom" but her friends will have her saved as "Carol"

Lightning: Watchtowers

Keeps your BTC secure on Lightning by monitoring the Bitcoin network

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

"All Lightning transactions are essentially multisignature bitcoin transactions (ie. requiring a predefined number of keys to spend, rather than just one). Typically these transactions are 2-of-2 multisigs with a timelock (aka. a withdrawal delay). When you move BTC around on the Lightning Network you're essentially sharing IOUs with people. When you eventually want to turn those IOUs into BTC in your wallet you need to ""close"" your Lightning channel. There's two general ways to move BTC out of a Lightning channel: 1. Holders of both keys choose to spend the BTC and agree on the final IOU in the chain of IOUs. You and the person you've sent or received money from each have one of them. Most LN transactions end this way as most parties simply obey the rules. 2. The other way is when the parties disagree, in this case 1 of the 2 keys can move the BTC with a timelock (ie. a delay). One party initiates the withdrawal, then the other party can see this transaction on the Bitcoin network and respond to it, either saying it's fine (ie. they've sent the right IOU), or that it's wrong and providing proof (in the form of more recent IOUs) that they are the rightful owner of the BTC. Watchtowers cover the second case, watching the Bitcoin network and making sure that the wrong transactions don't end up on the network sending money to the wrong people."

Watchtowers are already in a few wallets but they're still in alpha development

Eventually Watchtowers will be available everywhere and making fraudulent Lightning transactions by giving the Bitcoin network old IOUs will be practically impossible.

Currently Lightning channels are not yet easy to monitor, while the rate of fraud is low it is still feasible and does occasionally happen.

Lightning: Eltoo

Makes moving and protecting your BTC between Lightning and Bitcoin networks cheaper

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Go back and read about Watchtowers first. When watchtowers need to deal with stale IOUs like in the second case they may need to send a lot of them to the Bitcoin network to inform it of the final IOU in the chain. This process can create large transactions which are expensive. Eltoo allows for not having to do this making it much cheaper to prove the chain of IOUs to the Bitcoin network.

Eltoo is not currently feasible unless the Bitcoin network adds a new OPCODE called SIGHASH_NOINPUT If Eltoo is enabled it will make the use of Lightning and other technologies built on Bitcoin much more efficient and cheaper to operate and secure.

Lightning: Macaroons

Login to your favorite websites using Lightning

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Macaroons allow any service (not just websites) to provide you with a bearer token that you can use to identify yourself and optionally share with others. This way you can pay for a service with Lightning and later use that service at your leisure by providing it with your Macaroon to prove you paid previously.

It's in development and has recently made its way into a piece of Lightning software called LND.

Lightning: Onion Routing

Protects a Lightning transaction so no one else can see it

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Every Lightning IOU moves through multiple nodes (aka servers) on its way to its final recipient. For each node in its path another layer of encryption is added. When a node receives a Lightning IOU it is able to decrypt one, and only one of these layers before sending it on to the next node. This is a secure communication technology used in other software like Tor.

Onion routing is a part of the Lightning network and helps protect every transaction on it. However keep in mind that no single piece of technology is a magic solution to all issues and privacy on Lightning can and will be improved in a variety of ways.

Compare this to a current day credit card transaction where your data is regularly sold wholesale and you have no control over it. This is yet another technology that makes electronic transactions more like cash.

Lightning: Submarine Swaps

Makes moving BTC between the main Bitcoin network and Lightning easier for users

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Submarine Swaps bundle the entire process of moving BTC between Bitcoin and Lightning networks into one. This allows for seamless payments between BTC on the Bitcoin network and BTC IOUs on the Lightning network.

Submarine Swaps are already available in mobile wallets such as Phoenix Wallet, Breez, and Muun.

They're already here and they work.

Without Submarine Swaps if you have BTC on the Bitcoin network and wanted to send it over Lightning you would first need to make a transaction to create a Lightning channel. Then you would make a second transaction sending the BTC IOU over Lightning. This process is cumbersome and leads to friction in both the payment process and the UI of Lightning wallets. Now with submarine swaps the process is a single seamless step.

Lightning: Atomic Multi-path Payments

Allows for larger payments over Lightning

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

The naive way of sending money to friends on Lightning is to use BTC from a single Lightning channel. However every Lightning channel has a maximum size transaction that it can support. This improvement allows you to pay your friends and receive BTC from them using multiple Lightning channels, greatly increasing the amount of BTC that can be sent between you and your friends.

Schnorr

More private and cheaper Bitcoin transactions and smart contracts

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Essentially the way Bitcoin addresses and signatures (the things you use to authorize BTC transactions) work are not as efficient or private as they could be. Schnorr is a previously patented type of cryptographic (ie. electronic) signature that improves the privacy and cost of Bitcoin transactions; making them overall cheaper. Schnorr signatures are compiled from 1 or many keys and if they're used for a smart contract that includes multiple parties then it's impossible for any outsider to know what the smart contract did or could have done which means agreements between you and others are kept entirely private, looking just like any other transaction.

Schnorr is currently a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal at draft status which means it may make its way into the bitcoind software.

If Schnorr is activate on the network no one will be required to use it, but if they choose to then it will provide a more private and cheaper Bitcoin transaction experience. Long term it will help keep the cost to run the Bitcoin network software (aka. bitcoind) low meaning as many people as possible across the world will be able to be a part of the Bitcoin network while preserving the properties of the Bitcoin network.

Current transactions are generally bigger (ie. more expensive) and less private than Schnorr.

Taproot

More flexible and private Bitcoin smart contracts

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

Suppose you and 12 other business partners decide to collectively manage BTC for your startup. You all want an equal (or maybe unequal) say on what to spend your money on. You can store it all in one bank account and share a password, effectively trusting every person to not misbehave. Alternatively you can use a solution like Taproot to make sure you have equal (or unequal) say on when you can spend your BTC, effectively turning a business contract into an agreement enforced by the Bitcoin network. Taproot also improves privacy. Today if you and your business partners had such an agreement on the Bitcoin network and decided to spend your BTC on a new investment then the moment you did everyone on the Bitcoin network would know the wallets of every partner involved in the decision. This is bad if you don't want the world knowing the operations of your business. With Taproot (really Taproot + Schnorr + Taproot Scripts) you can make sure your transactions reveal much less or, in many cases, no information about your operations.

Taproot is currently a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal at draft status which means it may make its way into the bitcoind software.

If Taproot is activated all sorts of new types of applications and money management will be available and enforceable by the Bitcoin network. Taproot is very much a building block that may allow for types of applications no one has been able to build yet.

Currently we depend on many centrally controlled services to manage our money and agreements around them. Taproot will help us avoid or moderate the power that centralized intermediaries have over our money.

Miniscript

A language that makes it easier to write Bitcoin scripts/smart contracts

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

The Bitcoin network's programming language (Script) is a stack based language that is cumbersome to write with many edge cases. Miniscript makes it easy to write most Bitcoin scripts in a short bit of code (typically one line) with strong guarantees that your code will function correctly.

Check Template Verify

Allows for cost-efficient management of large amounts of transactions such as CoinJoins and Lightning channels

  • At a glance
  • Status
  • Further Reading

CTV is in many ways like Taproot, a more flexible and private way to use Bitcoin. Taproot covers the various things that can be done in a script while CTV lays out the pathway to do them. One interesting side-effect of this proposal is that it lets you store transactions so you can use scripts without needing direct access to your private keys.

CTV is currently a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal at draft status which means it may make its way into the bitcoind software.
